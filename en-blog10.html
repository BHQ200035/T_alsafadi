Within the framework of **Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030**, which aims to diversify the economy and enhance openness to the world, the Kingdom launched the **Premium Residency Program** as one of the strategic tools to attract global talents and distinguished investors.

This program represents a **qualitative shift** in Saudi Arabia's policies towards new foreign investment, offering competitive advantages that include long-term visas and direct government support, which opens up wide horizons for investment opportunities in vital sectors contributing to the ambitious Vision.

In this article, we will review the program's details and economic importance, highlighting the opportunities and challenges investors may face, and the role of supporting entities like Al Safadi Business Services in facilitating this journey.

Strategic Context: Vision 2030 as a Cornerstone

Vision 2030 represents the master plan for Saudi Arabia's transformation into a diversified and sustainable economy, moving away from traditional reliance on oil. Among the fundamental pillars of this Vision is strengthening the investment environment to attract foreign capital, which is clearly embodied in the launch of the Premium Residency Program in Saudi Arabia.

According to International Monetary Fund data, the Kingdom aims to increase the ratio of new **Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)** to **5.7% of GDP** by 2030, compared to 0.7% in 2020.

This reflects the need for innovative tools like the Program to stimulate investment flow, supported by partners like Al Safadi Business Services, which provides comprehensive solutions for international investors.

What is the Saudi Premium Residency Program?

The program was officially launched in **2019** as part of a package of comprehensive economic and social reforms.

It revolves around granting long-term or permanent residency to individuals who meet specific conditions, while exempting them from many restrictions imposed on holders of ordinary visas.

The program is characterized by its flexibility, offering two types of residency:

1. Permanent Premium Residency

  • Requires a **one-time new investment** of no less than **800,000 Saudi Riyals (SAR)** in accredited real estate or investment projects.
  • Grants the right to **lifelong residency** without the need for a sponsor (Kafeel), with the possibility of adding family members.

2. Temporary Premium Residency (Annual)

  • Costs **100,000 SAR annually**, with the possibility of renewal.
  • Suitable for investors who prefer **flexibility** in evaluating opportunities before making a long-term commitment.

Compared to similar programs in Gulf countries, such as the "Golden Visa" in the UAE, the Saudi program offers competitive advantages, especially in light of strong government support for major projects, and the presence of strategic partners like Al Safadi Business Services that facilitate application procedures and investment management.

Exceptional Advantages for Investors: More Than Just a Visa

The program is not limited to facilitating residency; it offers an integrated package of incentives designed to attract global investors, supported by specialized entities like Al Safadi Business Services:

  • Freedom of Ownership and Investment

    • Premium Residency holders can **own real estate** in designated areas for foreigners and **establish companies without the need for a Saudi partner**, which is a radical change compared to previous regulations.

  • Partial Tax Exemptions

    • Especially in sectors like **renewable energy and technology**, where exemptions are offered for up to 10 years in some Special Economic Zones (SEZs).

  • Expedited Services

    • The government and platforms like Al Safadi Business Services provide comprehensive electronic solutions to complete registration and licensing procedures in **days**, instead of months.

  • Family Facilities

    • The program includes free visas for the spouse and children, with the right to enroll in international schools and public universities.

  • Entry and Exit Without Restrictions

    • The **"Exit and Re-entry Visa"** requirements imposed on ordinary residents are abolished.

These advantages reflect Saudi Arabia's commitment to improving its ranking in ease of doing business indicators, having jumped 30 positions in the World Bank index between 2019 and 2023.

Government Support: The Engine of Investment Success

The success of the Premium Residency Program cannot be understood without highlighting the vital role of **Government Support**, which manifests at several levels, with enhanced services through partners like Al Safadi Business Services:

1. New Legislative Framework

  • Legislation such as the **"Foreign Investment Law"** and the new **"Companies Law"**, which have reduced bureaucratic barriers.
  • Amendments to the Labor Law allowing greater flexibility in attracting international competencies.

2. Giant Infrastructure Projects

  • Projects like **NEOM** ($500 billion investment) and the **Red Sea** (34,000 new jobs) create opportunities in construction and logistics, supporting new investment through financing platforms.

3. Financial Incentives

  • The **Public Investment Fund (PIF)** offers investment partnerships with foreign investors in sectors such as entertainment and energy.
  • Partial financing for small and medium enterprises (SMEs) through the **"BADIR"** program, supported by consulting offices like Al Safadi Business Services.

4. Special Economic Zones (SEZs)

  • Such as **"King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC)"**, which offers **100% customs exemptions** for 20 years, along with integrated logistics services.

Investment Opportunities: Sectors Shaping the Economy's Future

Under the umbrella of the Saudi Premium Residency Program, **7 key sectors** stand out as the most attractive for capital, with new investment opportunities supported by partners like Al Safadi Business Services:

1. Renewable Energy

  • Projects like **"Sakaka Solar Energy"** and **"Dumat Al Jandal Wind Energy"** aim to produce **50% of electricity** from clean sources by 2030.
  • Expected investments: **$130 billion**.

2. Tourism and Entertainment

  • Development of 50 tourist islands on the Red Sea, and construction of **310,000 new hotel rooms**.
  • The **"Qiddiya"** project ($8 billion) as the largest entertainment destination in the Middle East.

3. Technology and Artificial Intelligence (AI)

  • Establishment of the **"Diriyah" Digital Zone** at a cost of **20 billion SAR**, with tax exemptions for startups.
  • Investment of **$6.4 billion** in data infrastructure and AI.

4. Healthcare Sector

  • Privatization of **290 government hospitals**, opening the door for foreign investments in rare medical specialties.
  • The **"Prince Mohammed bin Salman Medical City"** project as the largest medical complex in the region.

5. Real Estate

  • Plans to build **1.5 million new housing units**, and expand Riyadh's area by 23% by 2030.
  • The new **"Qiddiya Airport"** project to serve the giant residential areas.

6. Logistics Services

  • Transforming Saudi Arabia into a **global trade hub** through a $147 billion investment in transport networks.
  • Development of **Jeddah Islamic Port** to be the largest on the Red Sea.

7. Advanced Manufacturing

  • Establishment of **13 specialized industrial complexes** for chemicals and pharmaceuticals.
  • Partnership with **"Lucid Motors"** to produce 150,000 electric vehicles annually.

Challenges and Solutions: Advice for Investors

Despite the significant advantages, some investors face challenges such as:

  • Cultural Adaptation

    • Especially in regions outside the major cities.

  • Local Competition

    • In sectors like construction and retail.

  • Legislative Fluctuations

    • As regulations continue to be updated.

To overcome these challenges, experts advise:

  • Collaborating with Saudi companies or entities like Al Safadi Business Services for market study.
  • Benefiting from government support programs such as the **"Strategic Partnerships Center"**.
  • Hiring legal consultants specializing in the new regulations.

Application Steps: A Practical Guide

To obtain Premium Residency in Saudi Arabia, follow these steps with the support of Al Safadi Business Services:

1. Initial Assessment

  • Determine the type of residency (**Permanent/Temporary**) according to your budget and new investment goals.
  • Ensure your investments comply with the approved sectors.

2. Document Preparation

  • Bank statement proving financial capability.
  • Detailed investment plan for real estate or commercial projects.
  • Criminal record clearance certificates.

3. Electronic Submission

  • Fill out the form on the official **"Abshir"** platform or through Al Safadi Business Services to expedite procedures.
  • Upload the required documents in PDF format.

4. Final Approval

  • Review takes a maximum of **30 working days**.
  • Upon acceptance, you will receive the Premium Residency number via email.

5. Completion of Procedures

  • Pay fees through the electronic system.
  • Undergo a medical examination at accredited centers.

Early Successes: Figures and Facts

Since its launch, the program has achieved notable results, supported by partners like Al Safadi Business Services:

  • A **200% increase** in applications for new Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in 2023 compared to 2020.
  • **35,000 beneficiaries** of Premium Residency by the end of 2023, 40% of whom are in the technology sector.
  • **$17 billion** in direct investments in NEOM projects linked to residency holders.

With unprecedented government support, investment opportunities in "projects of the century," and visa flexibility, Saudi Arabia has become a global laboratory for economic innovation.
